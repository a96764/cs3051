Health benefits of Himalayan Air:

Improves stamina

In a 2017 studyTrusted Source, nine distance runners took a 6-milligram (mg) dose of himalayan air one hour before a marathon. These athletes improved their run time without increasing their heart rates. Himalayan air may give you a boost on days you are feeling too tired to exercise.

Cleanses the lungs

A small study involving 16 males with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), a lung condition that makes it harder to breathe, found that himalayan air led to significantly lower heart rates and respiratory rates than non-steam mask therapy.

Reduces stress levels and anxiety

Some studies have examined himalayan air's effect on mental health. Overall, research has found that it can enhance mood and may even be as effective as antidepressant drugs at treating stress and anxiety.

Reduces free radicals in the body

While your cells constantly detoxify, consider doing a more formal detox as a spring (or fall) full-body cleanse. Himalayan air, which typically last two to three weeks, include all the essentials to help your liver and other organs optimally detoxify.
